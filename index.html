Session Notes (optional)
1
Single Stroke Speed Builder
Foundation · Priority block
3 min
  • Min 1–2: Speed builder sheet top to bottom at your current clean ceiling. Focus on even volume, both hands identical.
  • Min 3: Push +5 BPM, last line only. If it holds clean for the full minute, that becomes your new ceiling next session.
  • Tempo up only when the current level is solid — clean beats fast.
Target: 130 BPM 16th notes
2
Paradiddle Accents
Limb independence · Non-negotiable
3 min
  • Min 1: RLRR LRLL — accent beat 1 of each group. Rlrr Lrll.
  • Min 2: Shift accent to beat 3 — rlRr lrLl. Slow down if needed.
  • Min 3: Alternate both accent patterns every 4 bars.
  • Stay slow. 80 BPM clean beats 100 BPM sloppy every time.
This block moves the needle most — never skip it
3
Fill Simulation — 3+1
Musical context · Fills
2 min
  • Setup: Metronome at 105 BPM. Every click = 1 beat. 4 beats = 1 bar. You are simulating a real 4-bar phrase.
  • Beats 1–12 (bars 1, 2, 3 — the "3"): Tap your foot on beats 1 and 3 only. Play nothing on the pad. Hear the groove in your head. Stay locked to the metronome in silence.
  • Beats 13–16 (bar 4 — the "1"): Play your fill on the pad for exactly 4 beats (16 × 16th notes), then stop. Beat 1 of the next cycle starts immediately.
  • Repeat from the top — 3 silent bars, 1 fill bar, loop.
  • One fill per session, rotate daily:
    Mon/Thu: 16th note singles — RLRL RLRL RLRL RLRL (16 strokes, even volume)
    Tue/Fri: Paradiddle — RLRR LRLL RLRR LRLL
    Wed/Sat: Singles accented on beats 2 and 4 of the fill bar
  • Why this works: Most beginners practice fills in isolation — they sound fine alone but collapse in a song because the entry and exit timing breaks. The 3 silent bars force your brain to stay in tempo without playing. When bar 4 arrives, your fill starts exactly on time and lands back on beat 1 cleanly.
Target: clean fill entry and exit at 120 BPM
4
Double Stroke Endurance
Ceiling expansion · Rebound focus
2 min
  • RRLL RRLL — every 30 seconds add 5 BPM. Stop the moment the second stroke loses rebound, drop back 10 BPM and finish there.
  • The second stroke is pure rebound — do not muscle it. Let the stick bounce naturally.
  • Chase clean doubles, not the BPM number. Speed follows technique.
Target: clean doubles at 110 BPM
